	This book represents the content of a series of lectures given at Princeton University during the spring term of 1946, with minor modifications. In view of the fact that our knowledge of transcendental numbers is still very limited, it might be misleading to call it a theory of transcendental numbers. The book deals with several interesting special problems of transcendentality, but it is not merely a collection of scattered examples, since the methods contained in it may be helpful for finding more general results.

	Carl Ludwig Siegel.

	April, 1949. Princeton, New Jersey.
